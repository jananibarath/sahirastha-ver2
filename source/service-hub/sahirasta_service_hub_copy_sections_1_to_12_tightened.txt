Section 4 - Shares & Securities
Section title
Shares & Securities
Intro paragraph
This section covers share-related recovery and regularisation services. It is for investors, families, legal heirs, widows, senior citizens, and NRIs dealing with unclaimed dividends, shares transferred to IEPF, missing physical certificates, transmission after a death, or legacy holdings that still carry outdated details.
Support line
These issues often overlap. A family may discover old paper certificates after a death, find that records no longer match current documents, and then realise part of the holding has already moved to IEPF. The right route depends on what is wrong with the holding, how old the records are, and who is now entitled to claim.
Section CTA line
Explore the routes below and choose the one closest to your issue. If you are not sure which route applies, start with a consultation instead of guessing.
IEPF Claims
Recover shares, dividends, matured deposits, debentures, and other eligible amounts that have already moved to the Investor Education and Protection Fund because they remained unclaimed for too long.
Best for: investors or families discovering that dividends were never received and old holdings have moved out of direct control
Duplicate / Lost Share Documents
Use this route when original physical share certificates are lost, damaged, stolen, or no longer usable, and proof of ownership must be rebuilt before the holding can move further.
Best for: people with old paper shareholdings who cannot produce the original certificates
Transmission of Shares
Use this route when a shareholder has died and the shares must be moved to the nominee, surviving joint holder, legal heir, beneficiary, executor, or other entitled claimant.
Best for: widows, children, families, and legal heirs dealing with share ownership after a death
Physical Shares to Demat
Convert valid paper shareholdings into electronic form so the securities become active, traceable, and easier to manage, sell, consolidate, or pass on later.
Best for: holders of old physical shares that still exist but have never been dematerialised
Transmission-cum-Demat
Use this combined route where death-related ownership change and conversion of physical shares into demat form can sensibly be handled together in one coordinated process.
Best for: families dealing with physical share certificates left behind by a deceased holder
Name / Signature / Address Correction
Fix mismatches or outdated holder records such as name, signature, address, bank, PAN, or KYC-linked details when those defects block dividends, demat, transmission, duplicate issuance, or IEPF recovery.
Best for: investors whose holdings are valid but cannot move forward because the records no longer match current documents
Bridge paragraph
Many share-related matters involve more than one step. A missing certificate case may also need record correction. A post-death case may first require transmission and then demat. A holding may need to be traced before it can be claimed. Use the cards below as a starting point, not as hard silos.
Closing line
If you are unsure whether your case is IEPF, duplicate share documents, transmission, demat, or a record-correction issue, request a review and Sahirasta can help identify the right route.
Section 5 - Insurance Claims & Recovery
Section title
Insurance Claims & Recovery
Intro paragraph
This section covers insurance amounts and claim problems that are stuck, delayed, disputed, or blocked by documentation. It is for policyholders, nominees, widows, legal heirs, and families trying to recover money linked to life insurance or related policy benefits.
Support line
Insurance cases rarely fit into one box. A death claim may also involve a nominee mismatch, a lost policy bond, old bank details, policy-loan adjustment, or outdated insurer records. Start with the problem you can identify most clearly.
Section CTA line
Choose the block that best matches your issue. If the case cuts across more than one problem, begin with a consultation and we can identify the right claim path.
Unclaimed Maturity & Survival Benefits | Use this route when policy proceeds or scheduled payouts were due but never received, remain unclaimed, or are stuck because the final payout process was not completed. This includes maturity amounts, survival benefits, and other policy-linked payouts that should have been released but did not reach the claimant.
Death Claims & Family Support | Use this route when a policyholder has died and the nominee, widow, legal heir, or family member needs help filing, tracking, regularising, or following up on the claim. This route is especially relevant where the family is unsure what documents are needed or where the policy details are old or incomplete.
Rejected or Short-Settled Claims | Use this route when the insurer has denied the claim, delayed it without proper closure, or released less than expected and the matter needs review, clarification, representation, or escalation. It is meant for disputes where the claim amount itself is in question.
Policy Records, Bond, Nominee, KYC & Loan Issues | Use this route when payment is blocked because of a lost policy bond, nominee problem, outdated KYC, wrong address or bank details, policy-loan complication, or insurer records that do not match the claimant’s documents. This is the cleanup route that unblocks many stalled insurance cases.
Bridge paragraph
Insurance problems often overlap. A death claim may begin as a nominee issue and then turn into a records-mismatch problem. An unpaid maturity amount may also involve bank details, address updates, or policy servicing gaps. Use the blocks below to identify the main problem first.
Closing line
If you are not sure whether your issue is an unpaid benefit, a death claim, a rejected claim, a short settlement, or a policy-record problem, start with a consultation instead of guessing.
Section 6 - PF, Mutual Funds & Bank Deposits
Section title
PF, Mutual Funds & Bank Deposits
Intro paragraph
This section covers financial assets that are often forgotten, left inactive, or blocked because the holder changed jobs, shifted address, stopped tracking old investments, or died without leaving a clear record. It covers provident fund, mutual funds, and dormant bank deposits.
Support line
These cases are easy to miss because they do not always leave obvious paperwork at home. Families often discover them only during a claim review, succession process, job-history check, or broader asset search.
Section CTA line
Choose the block closest to your issue. If you are not sure whether the money sits with EPFO, a mutual fund or RTA, or a bank, start with a consultation instead of guessing.
Unclaimed Provident Fund
Use this route when PF money is stuck in an old employment-linked account, an old UAN/member ID trail is incomplete, or the family is trying to claim PF after the death of the account holder.
Unclaimed Mutual Funds
Use this route when folios are inactive, investments cannot be traced properly, redemption or transmission is blocked, or the family knows units exist but cannot access them easily.
Dormant / Inoperative Bank Deposits
Use this route when an account or deposit has become inactive, the bank relationship is old, the holder has died, or payout is blocked because of KYC, nomination, or reactivation issues.
Bridge paragraph
PF, mutual fund, and bank-deposit cases can overlap with succession issues, KYC correction, nominee gaps, and missing records. A family may first come looking for one asset and later discover several. Use the blocks below as the starting point.
Closing line
If you are unsure whether the issue is PF, mutual funds, or a dormant bank deposit, begin with a review of the available documents and the family or employment history. That is often the quickest way to identify the right route.

Section 11 - Pricing Snapshot
Sahirasta follows a pricing model that is straightforward in logic even when the underlying case is not. In most matters, the fee is generally around 10% of the value recovered, with the final fee depending on the complexity of the records, the nature of the claim, and the work involved in execution.
A straightforward correction case, a clean recovery matter, and a succession-heavy case with missing records should not all be priced the same way. The final commercial proposal is therefore shared after the broad facts are reviewed.
General fee logic — Fees are usually linked to the value recovered and the effort needed to move the case forward.
Complexity matters — Missing records, death-related claims, old physical documents, KYC mismatches, nominee disputes, and multi-stage execution can change the fee.
Clarity before work begins — The pricing approach should be explained before execution starts, so the client understands both the logic and the likely scope.
Closing line: If you want to understand how the fee may apply to your situation, review the Pricing page or request a consultation.
