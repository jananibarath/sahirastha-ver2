Content goal: Help visitors identify their problem quickly and see that Sahirasta handles messy real-world cases, not just clean textbook claims.
Section intro copy
Many people suspect money is due to them or their family. What stops them is not always the money - it is the confusion. Records are old, documents are missing, names do not match, nominations are outdated, and different institutions ask for different paperwork. Sahirasta helps identify the issue, organise the documents, and move the case forward.
IEPF claims
Shares and dividends may have moved to IEPF after years of inaction, even though the investor or family still has a valid claim.
Lost share documents and old physical shares
Physical certificates, folio details, or old records may be missing, damaged, or never converted into demat form.
Transmission after death
Families, widows, nominees, and legal heirs often need help establishing title and completing the paperwork after the death of an investor or policyholder.
Insurance claims delayed, rejected, or unpaid
Maturity amounts, death claims, survival benefits, or policy proceeds may remain unpaid because of missing records, nominee issues, KYC gaps, or claim disputes.
Unclaimed provident fund and mutual funds
Old employment-linked PF balances or mutual-fund folios may remain unattended for years because the account trail is incomplete or forgotten.
Dormant or inoperative bank deposits
Fixed deposits and bank balances can remain untouched for years, especially after relocation, family transitions, or the death of the account holder.
Correction and compliance blockers
Name mismatch, signature mismatch, address change, outdated bank details, missing nomination, or incomplete KYC can stop an otherwise valid claim.
NRI and cross-border cases
NRIs often face an extra layer of friction because the assets are in India but the claimant, documents, or bank details are abroad.

Section 10 — How Our Fees Work
Content goal: Reduce hesitation by showing the fee logic clearly and calmly without pretending that every case can be priced upfront.
Section intro copy
Sahirasta does not use vague promises or one-size-fits-all packages. Most matters in this space depend on the age of the records, the quality of the documents, whether succession issues are involved, and how much coordination is required across companies, registrars, insurers, banks, or government-linked systems. For many cases, the fee is generally around 10% of the value recovered. In some matters it may be lower or higher depending on complexity. The final fee is shared after the problem and available information are reviewed.
Pricing clarity note
This allows clients to understand the commercial approach before the work begins, while recognising that a straightforward recovery case and a messy legacy case do not require the same effort. If a matter needs a different fee structure because of unusual documentation gaps, succession issues, or other complexity, that will be explained at the proposal stage.
