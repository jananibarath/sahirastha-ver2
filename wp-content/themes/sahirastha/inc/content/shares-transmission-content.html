Transmission of Shares After Death

If shares are still in the name of a deceased holder, Sahirastha helps families, nominees, legal heirs, widows, joint holders, executors, and NRIs understand the transmission route and move the holding into a usable ownership record.

This is a succession-and-title service, not a voluntary transfer or sale route.

What Is This Service?

Transmission changes the ownership record after death so shares can pass to the legally entitled claimant set. The claimant may be a nominee, surviving joint holder, executor, administrator, or legal heir under the applicable route.

Transmission is different from transfer. Transfer is voluntary between living parties. Transmission is succession-based. Mixing the two often causes misclassification and delay.

When Does This Issue Usually Arise?

  • Family discovers shares after holder’s death.
  • Investments exist but are still recorded in deceased person’s name.
  • No nomination, or nomination does not settle practical entitlement.
  • Multiple heirs are involved and route is unclear.
  • Transmission is needed before duplicate-document, demat, or IEPF actions can proceed.

Who Typically Needs Help?

  • Nominees and surviving joint holders.
  • Widows, spouses, children, parents, siblings, and other legal heirs.
  • Executors/administrators under will or court-backed route.
  • NRIs inheriting India-based holdings.

How Sahirastha Helps

We identify the actual holding pattern and succession path first: physical or demat mode, nominee position, joint-holder status, and whether will/probate/succession/heirship route applies. Then we structure records so identity, relationship, entitlement, banking, demat destination, and signatures tell one consistent story.

Where the matter overlaps with missing certificates, demat conversion, record correction, or IEPF, transmission is coordinated with the next step rather than run in isolation.

Documents Usually Required

  • Death certificate of deceased holder.
  • PAN, identity, and address proof of claimant(s).
  • Demat account details / Client Master List for final credit route.
  • Bank proof where payout-linked processing applies.
  • Transmission request formats prescribed for the case.
  • Original share certificates in physical-holding files.
  • Nomination/joint-holder evidence where applicable.
  • Will, probate, succession certificate, legal-heir support, indemnity/affidavit/NOC set as route requires.
  • NRI overseas identity and execution support where relevant.

Why Transmission Cases Get Delayed

  • Route confusion where no nomination exists or multiple heirs are present.
  • Entitlement documents do not align.
  • Family conflict or inconsistent declarations.
  • Fragmented holdings across companies/registrars/folios.
  • Missing or damaged physical papers.
  • Name/signature/address/bank mismatches in legacy records.
  • Weak overseas execution trail for NRI claimants.

Special Notes for NRIs, Legal Heirs, and Families

NRI cases need disciplined execution, identity consistency, attestation, and account alignment. For families, the key challenge is often choosing the correct claimant route—not just collecting documents. A staged approach usually works better than trying to solve the whole estate in one pass.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Is transmission the same as transfer? No, transmission is death-triggered succession; transfer is voluntary between living parties.
  • Can transmission proceed without nomination? Often yes, with the correct entitlement route and documents.
  • What if there are multiple heirs? Case can proceed, but route and document consistency become more important.
  • Can physical shares be transmitted? Yes, and they may later need demat or duplicate-document support.
  • Can NRIs complete transmission from abroad? Yes, with stronger execution and documentary alignment.
