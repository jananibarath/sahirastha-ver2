Convert Old Physical Share Certificates into Demat Form

If you still hold paper share certificates, Sahirastha helps you move them into a usable demat account so the holding can be operated, corrected, or prepared for later sale, transmission, or claim work.

We assist investors, NRIs, senior citizens, and families dealing with old paper holdings, incomplete records, registrar issues, and legacy folios.

What Does Physical Shares to Demat Mean?

This is the conversion of paper certificates into electronic holdings credited to a demat account. Operationally, certificate details, folio data, signatures, identity records, and demat account pattern must align well enough for processing.

Where papers are damaged, details are mismatched, or holder is deceased, the case may also require correction or transmission work before demat can move.

When Does This Issue Usually Arise?

  • Old physical certificates discovered in family records or lockers.
  • Investor wants to operate/sell shares and finds holding is still in paper form.
  • Demat account exists but legacy conversion was never completed.
  • Holder details no longer match current identity/KYC/bank trail.
  • Deceased-holder case is initially mistaken as plain demat conversion.

Who Typically Needs Help?

  • Investors with legacy paper holdings.
  • Senior citizens with long-held undematerialised certificates.
  • Families unsure whether discovered certificates are process-ready.
  • NRIs with old India-based paper holdings requiring alignment.
  • Mixed cases containing correction/duplicate/succession layers.

How Sahirastha Helps

We first classify whether the file is clean dematerialisation or mixed-route (correction, duplicate-document, succession, or transmission-cum-demat). Documents Usually Required

  • Original physical share certificates.
  • PAN, identity, and address proof of holder/claimant.
  • Client Master List / demat account details for credit.
  • Dematerialisation request/service request formats as applicable.
  • Bank proof where required.
  • Signature verification / banker attestation for signature mismatch.
  • Name/address/KYC correction documents for outdated folio records.
  • Succession/entitlement support in deceased-holder cases.

Why Cases Get Delayed or Stuck

  • Damaged/illegible certificates or fragmented folio records.
  • Mismatched holder name/signature/address/PAN/KYC.
  • Demat account details not aligned with holder pattern.
  • Assumption that shares are instantly sale-ready without regularisation.
  • Case actually requires transmission or transmission-cum-demat.
  • Multiple certificates/registrars complicate document chain.

Special Notes for NRIs, Legal Heirs, and Families

NRI files typically hinge on documentary alignment and execution quality. Legal-heir families often misclassify deceased-holder cases as simple demat conversion; correct route selection early saves significant delay.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Can old certificates still be converted? Often yes, if holding is valid and route is prepared correctly.
  • Can I sell physical shares directly? In most practical cases, holdings are regularised into demat first.
  • What if certificates are very old/damaged? Age alone is not fatal, but damaged or inconsistent papers need closer review.
  • What if name/signature changed? Correction support is often required alongside demat conversion.
  • What if original holder has died? Route often shifts to transmission or transmission-cum-demat.
