Transfer Shares from a Deceased Holder and Move Them into Demat Form

If old physical certificates are still in the name of a deceased investor, Sahirastha helps legal heirs, nominees, widows, families, executors, and NRIs handle the combined transmission-and-demat route.

This is a combined succession-and-conversion service used when ownership transmission and dematerialisation must both happen before holdings become usable.

What Does Transmission-cum-Demat Mean?

Two layers must be completed together: entitlement after death, and conversion of paper certificates to electronic form. If either layer is weak, the file usually stalls.

When Does This Issue Usually Arise?

  • Family finds old physical certificates after death.
  • Transmission was never completed and holding remained paper-based.
  • Family wants to sell/consolidate but shares are still paper + deceased-name.
  • Demat account exists but legal entitlement route is unclear.
  • No nominee or nominee trail does not resolve heir-level entitlement.
  • Identity/signature/address records are outdated.

Who Typically Needs This Service?

  • Widows, children, nominees, and legal heirs with inherited holdings.
  • Families regularising long-pending estates.
  • NRIs inheriting Indian paper holdings.
  • Multi-heir families requiring coordinated documentation.
  • Claimants unsure whether case is transmission, demat, duplicate, or correction-led.

How Sahirastha Helps

We classify the case before filing: holder pattern, certificate availability, nominee/heir route, final receiving demat pattern, and any hidden duplicate/correction/signature issues. Then we build the transmission and demat file in the correct order and guide follow-up if objections arise.

Documents Usually Required

  • Death certificate of original holder.
  • Original physical certificates (if available).
  • Nominee/succession/legal-heir/probate/affidavit support based on route.
  • PAN, identity, and address proof of claimant(s).
  • Receiving demat account details / Client Master List.
  • Bank proof where verification requires it.
  • Signature verification support where legacy signatures mismatch.
  • Name/address/KYC rectification support for outdated folio data.
  • Registrar-specific declarations and indemnities where required.

Why Cases Get Delayed or Stuck

  • Case treated as plain demat conversion instead of succession-led route.
  • No nominee or incomplete heir coordination/consent trail.
  • Old/damaged certificates not cleanly mapped to current records.
  • Mismatch in name/address/PAN/signature/KYC chain.
  • Receiving demat account pattern not aligned to final claimant route.
  • Hidden duplicate/correction issues discovered mid-process.
  • Long delay makes records harder to rebuild.

Special Notes for NRIs, Legal Heirs, and Families

NRI cases require high execution discipline across identity, attestation, bank route, and demat alignment. Multi-heir families need one clear documented route; informal family understanding is not enough for processing.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • How is this different from transmission or demat alone? Transmission changes ownership after death; demat converts paper to electronic; transmission-cum-demat combines both in one route.
  • Can old death cases still proceed? Often yes, though older files may require more reconstruction.
  • What if we have certificates but no nominee? Route may still proceed via heir/succession documentation.
  • Can NRIs complete this from abroad? Yes, with careful documentation and execution.
  • Can shares be sold immediately after discovery? Usually no; ownership and format regularisation must complete first.
